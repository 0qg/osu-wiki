**NOTE: IF YOU DON'T HAVE SOME KNOWLEDGE OF CHINESE, YOU MAY FEEL IT DIFFICULT TO READ.**

Qianese, in communication groups of Chinese osu! players, are mainly the words and their derivations that are used by a certain player called "钱老板 (Qian Laoban, Boss Qian)". Qianese are incomprehensible because they are typos made by Qian Laoban when chatting. But later some of the typos gradually evolved to conventional expressions, and they were spread to music-game players, Japanese mah-jong players (mostly activities via Internet) and even Weibo (micro blog) users.

Origin
------

Since most of the users in mainland China use full Pinyin input method (and Pinyin abbreviations), generally speaking, the premise of the production of Qianese is full Pinyin input method. The typos produced by Wubi (five-stroke), Cangjie and double Pinyin input methods will not be within the scope of discussion, because they are used by only a small group of users.

### 青玉案·屙屎

则不呢？锟斤拷屯屯。热爆挖鼻烫烫烫，别墅奥尔良。

你诗人？成书店爷爷。一圈打包疼疼疼，柑橘峨嵋酒 (Note: gan **ju e** mei jiu)。

Notes:

-   From Floor 13373 of a thread in Baidu osu Bar. [6](http://tieba.baidu.com/p/2804083720?pn=425);
-   **烫烫烫**, **屯屯屯** are common messy codes that may appear when using Visual C++ Debug mode to compile programs in Chinese–Simplified Windows operating system, the origins of which are **0xcccccccc** and **0xcdcdcdcd**;
-   **锟斤拷** is a result of the compile errors of some programs or websites. Unicode character U+FFFD will be **\\xef\\xbb\\xbf** in UTF-8. Analyse them in GBK code, and you will get 锟(**\\xef\\xbb**) 斤(**\\xbf\\xef**) 拷(**\\xbb\\xbf**);
-   Actually, the ci (poem) doesn't follow the name of the tunes Qing Yu An. [7](http://longyusheng.org/cipai/cipai82.html) From the sentence structure, it is more like Yi Jiangnan (Memories of the South). [8](http://longyusheng.org/cipai/cipai4.html)
